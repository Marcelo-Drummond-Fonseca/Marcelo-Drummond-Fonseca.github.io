Fire Emblem

A few facts about Fire Emblem

Fire Emblem is a fantasy tactical role-playing game franchise developed by Intelligent Systems and published by Nintendo. First produced and published for the Famicom in 1990, the series currently consists of sixteen core entries and four spinoffs. Gameplay revolves around the tactical movement of characters across grid-based environments, while incorporating a story and characters similar to traditional role-playing video games.

About Fire Emblem

  • Number of Games: 13 core series games, 3 remakes and 4 spinoff titles.
  • Total Sales: over 10 million copies of games in the Fire Emblem series have been sold worldwide.
  • The series is still continuing to this day, with the most recent installmente, Fire Emblem: Engage, being announced on September 13th, 2022

Top 3 facts about Fire Emblem

  1. Fire Emblem is the best selling Strategy RPG series of all time. Its best-selling game, Fire Emblem: Three Houses, is the best-selling single Strategy Game of all time.
  2. Fire Emblem wasn't brought to western audiences until its 7th installment, which led to it being simply named "Fire Emblem".
  3. The Fire Emblem Series was nearly cancelled after poor sales on successive installments. However, the 13th game in the series, Fire Emblem: Awakening, had such a positive reception that the series was able to continue
